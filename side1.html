Today, we will talk about backpacks.









Backpacks play a significant role in posture problems, especially among children, because they affect the spine while it is still developing. This can result in:
  • Lower back pain
  • Shoulder pain
  • Neck pain
  • Headaches
  • Difficulty concentrating in the school
  • Rounded shoulder
  • Kyphosis and/or scoliosis
  • Spondylosis
Now that you know what a backpack can do, let me tell you the characteristics of an ideal backpack:
  • The backpack should not weigh more than 10% of the body weight. For example, if the child weighs 25 kg, the backpack should not exceed 2.25 kg.
  • The backpack should be suitable for the body size.
  • The backpack should be padded at the shoulders and back.
  • The backpack should have long straps to lower it to the level of the lower back, keeping it close to the body's center of gravity.
  • The backpack should be worn on both shoulders, not just one.
  • The backpack should have a front strap to distribute the weight evenly across the body.
  • The backpack should have multiple compartments to help distribute the weight.
By following these guidelines, we can choose the right backpack. Choose your backpack wisely,
and remember that prevention is better than cure.
